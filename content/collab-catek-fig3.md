Catek et al. (2022) Figure 3: Behavioral results for the no-swap (N), motion-swap (M), and color-swap (C) conditions. Bars show percent correct for cued and uncued trials; error bars are 95% CIs across observers (n=15). The cueing advantage (cued > uncued) is present in all three conditions, indicating that neither the motion-swap nor the color-swap eliminates the delayed-onset advantage. The VRDots replication (right) tests a combined motion+color swap (MC) at Catek-matched aperture and density parameters.
