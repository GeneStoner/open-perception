For those curious about the underlying science, here is a selection of relevant papers organized by topic.

**Introductory / Conceptual**

- [Duncan (1984)](https://doi.org/10.1037/0096-3445.113.4.501) — *Selective attention and the organization of visual information.* The foundational paper establishing objects as units of attentional selection.
- [Cavanagh (2011)](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.visres.2011.01.015) — *Visual Cognition.* Vision Research, 51(13), 1538–1551. An accessible review of object-based attention and its relationship to visual cognition more broadly.

**Transparent Motion and Superimposed Stimuli**

- [Valdés-Sosa, Cobo & Pinilla (2000)](https://doi.org/10.1037/0096-1523.26.2.488) — *Attention to object files defined by transparent motion.* Demonstrates that transparently overlapping motion fields define distinct attentional objects.
- [Reynolds, Alborzian & Stoner (2003)](https://doi.org/10.1016/S0042-6989%2802%2900403-0) — *Exogenously cued attention triggers competitive selection of surfaces.* Vision Research, 43(1), 59–66.
- [Stoner & Blanc (2010)](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.visres.2009.11.015) — *Exploring the mechanisms underlying surface-based stimulus selection.* Vision Research, 50(2), 229–238.
- [Catek, Özkan, Kafaligonul & Stoner (2022)](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.cortex.2022.02.013) — *Behavioral and ERP evidence that object-based attention utilizes fine-grained spatial mechanisms.* Cortex, 151, 89–104.

**Neural / ERP / Physiological Studies**

- [O'Craven, Downing & Kanwisher (1999)](https://doi.org/10.1038/44134) — *fMRI evidence for objects as the units of attentional selection.* Nature, 401, 584–587.
- [Hillyard, Vogel & Luck (1998)](https://doi.org/10.1098/rstb.1998.0281) — *Sensory gain control as a mechanism of selective attention: electrophysiological and neuroimaging evidence.*
- [Hillyard & Anllo-Vento (1998)](https://doi.org/10.1073/pnas.95.3.781) — *Event-related brain potentials in the study of visual selective attention.*
- [Wannig, Rodríguez & Freiwald (2007)](https://doi.org/10.1038/nn1903) — *Attention to surfaces modulates motion processing in extrastriate area MT.* Nature Neuroscience, 10, 803–809.
- [Roelfsema (2006)](https://doi.org/10.1146/annurev.neuro.29.051605.112939) — *Cortical algorithms for perceptual grouping.* Annual Review of Neuroscience, 29, 203–227.

**Broader Reviews**

- [Scholl (2001)](https://doi.org/10.1016/S0010-0277%2800%2900152-9) — *Objects and attention: The state of the art.* Cognition, 80, 1–46. A comprehensive review of the field.
- [Chen (2012)](https://doi.org/10.3758/s13414-012-0322-z) — *Object-based attention: A tutorial review.* Attention, Perception, & Psychophysics, 74, 784–802.
- [Frontiers Review (2016)](https://doi.org/10.3389/fnint.2016.00039) — *Mechanisms of Object-Based Attention.*
