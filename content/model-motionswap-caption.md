**Motion swap — our replication of Stoner & Blanc (2010), Fig. 4.** At translation onset the two fields exchange rotation directions: the **test field** (dashed) reverses from clockwise to counter-clockwise, and the **competitor** (solid) reverses the other way. Crucially, each field **keeps its identity** (line style) through the feature change. The consequence for the model is decisive: after the swap, the direction-channel content of a *cued-swap* trial becomes identical to that of an *uncued-no-swap* trial — so a model that selects on motion direction alone has no choice but to predict that the cueing advantage **reverses**. The behavioral data show no such reversal (see the response figure), which is the model's clean falsification and the motivation for surface-identity selection.
