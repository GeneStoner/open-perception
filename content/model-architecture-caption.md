**The circuit.** Two direction-selective channels — one tuned to each field's rotation — each carry a slow self-adaptation loop (firing rate feeds back as subtractive self-inhibition). Both channels project as divisive, normalizing inhibition onto a single translation detector, which is excited by the brief coherent shift during the translation window. The detector's output is a Naka–Rushton ratio, *R*₍TD₎ = *KE* / (*E* + *I* + σ). There is no attention term — the entire cued/uncued asymmetry must come from adaptation. *Equations and parameters are taken directly from Appendix A of the paper.*
