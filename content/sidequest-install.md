These instructions walk you through installing a VRDots experiment APK on your Meta Quest 3 headset. You will do this once to set up your headset, then repeat only the final step for each new experiment build.

**What you will need:** a Meta Quest 3 headset, a USB-C cable, and a computer (Mac or Windows).

**Step 1 — Create a Meta Developer Account**

Go to [developer.oculus.com](https://developer.oculus.com) and sign in with the same account linked to your Quest. Create an organization (the name does not matter). This unlocks Developer Mode on your headset.

**Step 2 — Enable Developer Mode on your Quest**

Open the **Meta Quest** app on your phone. Go to Menu → Devices → select your headset → Developer Mode, and toggle it on. Restart your headset.

**Step 3 — Install SideQuest on your computer**

Download and install **SideQuest (Advanced Installer)** from [sidequestvr.com](https://sidequestvr.com/setup-howto). This is a free desktop app for sideloading APKs.

**Step 4 — Connect your headset**

Plug your Quest into your computer via USB-C. Put on the headset — you will see a prompt asking *Allow USB debugging?* Select **Always allow from this computer** and confirm.

**Step 5 — Verify the connection**

In SideQuest, the top bar should show a green dot and your headset name. If it shows red, try unplugging and reconnecting, or use a different cable (some cables are charge-only).

**Step 6 — Install the APK**

In SideQuest, click the **Install APK** button (box with a down arrow, top-right). Select the `.apk` file you downloaded. Installation takes about 30 seconds.

**Step 7 — Launch the experiment**

On your Quest, go to **App Library → Unknown Sources**. You will find the VRDots app listed there. Launch it and follow any on-screen instructions.

**Troubleshooting**

If the app does not appear in Unknown Sources, try restarting the headset after installation. If SideQuest cannot connect, confirm Developer Mode is enabled and that you accepted the USB debugging prompt inside the headset.
