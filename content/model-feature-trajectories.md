**Feature trajectories of the two dot fields (after Stoner & Blanc, 2010, Fig. 4).** Each panel shows the motion direction of both fields — clockwise (**CW**), translation (**TRANS**), or counter-clockwise (**CCW**) — across the trial. *Line style* marks **field identity**: the **dotted** line is the field whose dots briefly translate, the **solid** line is its competitor. *Line color* is the field's **dot color**. **Left column (A, C):** no motion swap — the translating field keeps its original rotation. **Right column (B, D):** motion swap — at translation onset the non-translating field reverses its rotation, and afterward the translating field adopts the competitor's earlier direction. Because the swap exchanges which field carries which rotation, a *cued* translation (the delayed-onset dots translating, A) becomes the functional *uncued* case under the swap (D), and vice versa (C ↔ B) — the basis for the model's predicted reversal. The gray band marks the 40 ms translation; the brief onset offset (≈ 750 ms) is the delayed onset of the second field.
