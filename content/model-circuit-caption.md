**The model as a biased-competition circuit (Reynolds, Chelazzi & Desimone, 1999).** The translation detector is a single neuron in **MT / MST** — a region whose large receptive fields take in both transparent surfaces at once. It receives **excitatory** drive from the population signalling coherent translation, and **divisive (normalizing) inhibition**, through local interneurons, from the populations signalling the two fields' rotation. Its steady-state response, *R*₍TD₎ = *KE* / (*E* + *I* + σ), is exactly Eq. 4 of Reynolds et al. — the same biased-competition computation they recorded in V2/V4, and which Recanzone et al. (1997) and Treue & Martinez-Trujillo / Maunsell (1996) showed operating in the motion areas MT/MST. The rotating fields are the *competitors* that suppress the detector; what **biases** the competition is either the adaptation difference between the two rotation inputs (Stoner & Blanc, 2010 — our current model) or attention (Reynolds & Heeger, 2009). The direction-selective inputs are supplied by V1→MT; the competition itself is an MT/MST-scale phenomenon, because that is where the two surfaces share a receptive field.
